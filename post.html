Breakfast Recipes

Baked Steel Cut Oatmeal

This is a great basic recipe but what I love about baked steel cut oats is that you can make so many different varieties! You can add fruits like berries, apples, bananas, nuts, or even some grated zucchini!

Part of what makes this meal so nutritious is the steel cut oats. They are different from regular oats in that they contain the entire oat kernel - the bran, the germ, and the endosperm. The healthy fiber, minerals, vitamins, antioxidants and phytochemicals in whole grains are mainly found in the bran and germ parts of the grain. You miss out on these parts with refined grains.

Nutritional benefits of whole grains:

  • Reduce risk of diabetes and obesity
  • Reduce risk of heart disease
  • Reduce risk of some cancers
  • Lowers cholesterol
  • Supports the gut microbiota

Mini Pumpkin Waffles

A perfect fall breakfast that is insanely easy to make!

Nutritional benefits of pumpkin:

  • High in vitamins A, C, and E
    • All three of these vitamins have antioxidant properties that support the immune system
  • High in potassium which helps reduce high blood pressure
  • High in manganese which has anti-inflammatory properties

Apple Cinnamon Quinoa Breakfast Bake

This recipe has a short list of ingredients yet is packed with nutrients with your whole grains, fruit, and protein! I love making bakes like these because it supplies me breakfast throughout the week!

Nutritional benefits:

  • Apples
    • High in fiber which is important for gut health
  • Cinnamon
    • Anti-inflammatory
    • Rich in antioxidants which helps reduce risk of certain diseases and helps brain function
    • Heart health
  • Quinoa
    • Complete protein meaning it contains all 9 essential amino acids that our body needs to build proteins
    • Rich in calcium and vitamin E
    • Whole grain that is rich in fiber
